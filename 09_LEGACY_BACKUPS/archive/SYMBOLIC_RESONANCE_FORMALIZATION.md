$D_{\omega_j}$: Diffusion operators parameterized by frequency $\omega_j$ - The algebra is generated under non-commutative composition ## Theorem 1: Fundamental Non-Commutativity **Statement**: For distinct resonance parameters $\phi_1, \phi_2$ and frequencies $\omega_1, \omega_2$, the operators satisfy: $$[R_{\phi_1}, D_{\omega_2}] = R_{\phi_1} D_{\omega_2} - D_{\omega_2} R_{\phi_1} \neq 0$$ **Proof**: Consider the action on a test function $f \in L^2(\mathbb{R})$: $$R_{\phi_1} D_{\omega_2} f(x) = e^{i\phi_1} \int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{-|x-y|^2/\sigma_2^2} e^{i\omega_2 y} f(y) dy$$ $$D_{\omega_2} R_{\phi_1} f(x) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{-|x-y|^2/\sigma_2^2} e^{i\omega_2 y} [e^{i\phi_1} f(y)] dy$$ The commutator becomes: $$[R_{\phi_1}, D_{\omega_2}] f(x) = e^{i\phi_1} \int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{-|x-y|^2/\sigma_2^2} f(y) [e^{i\omega_2 y} - e^{i\omega_2 x}] dy$$ **Non-zero condition**: $\omega_2 \neq 0$ and $\phi_1 \neq 0$ (mod $2\pi$) ## Theorem 2: Non-Reducibility Conditions **Statement**: The symbolic resonance algebra $\mathcal{R}_\Phi$ is **non-reducible** (cannot be decomposed into simpler algebras) if and only if: 1. **Frequency Incommensurability**: $\{\omega_i\}$ contains at least two rationally independent frequencies 2. **Phase Non-Degeneracy**: $\{\phi_i\}$ spans a dense subset of $\mathbb{T}$ (the circle group) 3. **Coupling Condition**: $\exists i,j$ such that $\gcd(\lfloor\omega_i/\epsilon\rfloor, \lfloor\phi_j/\epsilon\rfloor) = 1$ for sufficiently small $\epsilon > 0$ **Proof Sketch**: Use Weyl's equidistribution theorem combined with spectral analysis of the commutator algebra. ## Theorem 3: Computational Universality **Statement**: Symbolic Resonance Computing with parameters satisfying the non-reducibility conditions is **computationally universal** for the class of problems involving: -
